Hear Me Now: The Black Potters of Old Edge, South Carolina

By Daidrian Hammond

In the decades before the Civil War, a successful industry developed in Old Edgefield District, South Carolina. The area was rich in clay, making it the perfect place for a stoneware industry. Enslaved African Americans were the pinnacle of the labor-intensive industry.

The stoneware enslaved African Americans made supported Old Edgefield, South Carolina expanding population through creative and stylistic innovations of pottery. The stoneware also linked to the plantation economy that carried the South's economic weight in the country.
